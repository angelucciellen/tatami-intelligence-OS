### 11.1 Core Philosophy

**Principle:** Visual market cartography, not narrative analysis.

The brain processes patterns visually, not through text. Each trading session, you will:

1. Generate or receive a **Macro Intelligence Layer brief** (catalysts, regime, expected patterns)
2. Capture a **live chart screenshot** (current price location)
3. Submit both here
4. Receive a **visual strategy map** showing where your macro thesis plays out on the structure

No text reports. No predictions. Only visual zones, probabilities, and IF/THEN decision points.

---

### 11.2 Workflow

**Daily Input (for each trading session):**
- Macro Intelligence Brief (regime, catalysts, IF/THEN, expected engine states)
- Live chart screenshot (5-minute timeframe preferred)
- Current session context (time, instruments, binary events)

**Immediate Output:**
- Visual 5m chart with annotated zones
- Probability map (green/yellow/red)
- Narrative levels (what each price level means)
- Trade blueprints (3 scenarios, IF/THEN only)
- Engine translation (macro → engine state prediction)
- Chart annotation guide (exactly what to draw)

---

### 11.3 Visual Output Structure

#### SECTION 1: MARKET STORY
Plain English: What is happening? Who controls? What is the dominant narrative? Why is price behaving this way? (5 bullet points maximum, no fluff.)

#### SECTION 2: PROBABILITY MAP
Mark on chart:
- 🟩 High Probability Long Zones (with % and confirmation rules)
- 🟥 High Probability Short Zones (with % and confirmation rules)
- 🟨 Neutral / Wait Zones (with % and confirmation rules)
- ⚠ Invalidation Zones (where macro thesis breaks)

#### SECTION 3: NARRATIVE LEVELS
Every important price level translated into behavioral meaning.

Example:
- 30,300 = Buyers proving acceptance
- 30,500 = Momentum continuation
- 30,700 = Exhaustion risk
- 30,000 = Narrative failure

#### SECTION 4: TRADE BLUEPRINTS
Up to 3 scenarios (A/B/C), each with:
- Bias (with probability)
- Entry Zone
- Stop
- Target 1
- Target 2
- Probability (conditional IF/THEN only, never absolute)

#### SECTION 5: ENGINE TRANSLATION
Convert macro context into likely engine behavior.

Example:
- COMPRESSION likely (reason: FOMC psychology)
- TRAVERSAL probability increasing (reason: uncertainty cleared)
- DISTORTION risk elevated (reason: deal collapse binary)
- EXHAUSTION expected near resistance (reason: profit-taking)

#### SECTION 6: CHART ANNOTATION
Exact instructions for what to draw on chart.

Example:
- Draw red zone between X and Y. Label: "Macro Resistance"
- Draw green zone between X and Y. Label: "Acceptance Area"
- Draw arrow from X toward Y. Label: "Continuation Path"
- Draw invalidation line at X. Label: "Narrative Failure"

### 12.1 Pattern Definitions & Visual Markers

**P1 — Initial Impulse**

**Definition:** First directional move after consolidation or news catalyst. Strong conviction, minimal wick rejection.

**Where it appears:** Early in session after gap or after macro surprise

**Macro trigger:** News release, geopolitical event, earnings, central bank decision

**Visual markers:**
- Large candle body in one direction
- Minimal lower wick (if upward) or upper wick (if downward)
- Volume confirmation visible

**Entry signal:** First pullback after impulse completes (support/resistance established)

**Exit signal:** If impulse stalls before reaching expected target

**Engine state:** Early TRAVERSAL or EXPANSION

**Example:** NQ gap-up on Iran deal Mon Jun 16 — P1 impulse established at open

---

**P2 — Continuation Bar**

**Definition:** Bar that closes in same direction as prior bar, no reversal wick, small/neutral body. Confirms direction without extending.

**Where it appears:** Within trending sequences, after breakouts

**Macro trigger:** Confirmation of macro thesis, no new catalysts

**Visual markers:**
- Small body relative to prior candle
- Same close direction
- Minimal or no wick rejection

**Entry signal:** Stack of 3+ continuation bars = pattern probability high

**Exit signal:** Reversal wick or body direction change

**Engine state:** TRAVERSAL or COMPRESSION

**Example:** Memory zone holds, buyers proving acceptance over multiple 5m bars

---

**P3 — Momentum Continuation**

**Definition:** Multi-bar sequence where each close is higher than prior, accelerating. Strong conviction in direction.

**Where it appears:** After PRIMARY entry, confirmation of TRAVERSAL state

**Macro trigger:** Macro thesis unfolding without dispute, all signals aligned

**Visual markers:**
- Each bar close > prior bar close
- Growing volume or at least sustained
- Minimal upper wicks
- Candlesticks stepping upward visually

**Entry signal:** Entry into P3 sequence itself (not the start, but confirmation after 3-4 bars establish pattern)

**Exit signal:** First bar that doesn't close higher than prior, OR reaches target

**Engine state:** TRAVERSAL dominant

**Probability in brief:** HIGH if macro catalyst is unambiguous (deal confirmed, no Trump denial)

**Example:** NQ 30,480 → 30,545 → 30,620 — three-bar momentum run on deal confirmation

---

**P4 — Reversal Rejection**

**Definition:** Bar that closes opposite direction from expected, often with wick extending into prior range. Signals rejection of price level.

**Where it appears:** At invalidation zones, at support/resistance tests that fail

**Macro trigger:** Deal dispute, Trump tweet, unexpected data, economic surprise

**Visual markers:**
- Long wick in one direction
- Close in opposite direction
- High rejection probability if wick extends 1-2 ATR

**Entry signal:** Reversal rejection bars are SELL signals if they reject upside. BUY if they reject downside at support.

**Exit signal:** If reversal bar holds and next bar confirms rejection direction

**Engine state:** FAILED or DISTORTION emerging

**Probability in brief:** MEDIUM if binary event risk is high (FOMC, deal signing)

**Example:** NQ spike to 30,700 but closes below 30,650 = reversal rejection at exhaustion zone

---

**P5 — Consolidation Range**

**Definition:** Series of bars oscillating within a tight range (ATR < 0.5x normal). Indecision, accumulation or distribution.

**Where it appears:** Before breakouts, after strong moves, into binary events

**Macro trigger:** Macro uncertainty building, traders waiting for catalyst

**Visual markers:**
- Tight range, candles small-bodied
- Multiple touches of support/resistance within range
- Volume may reduce or stay flat

**Entry signal:** Breakout from consolidation = strong signal (P6)

**Exit signal:** If consolidation expands after initial breakout false (usually 1-2 bars confirm true breakout)

**Engine state:** COMPRESSION

**Probability in brief:** HIGH into FOMC or signing events (traders waiting)

**Example:** Pre-FOMC Wed trading range 30,620–30,680, waiting for decision

---

**P6 — Breakout from Consolidation**

**Definition:** First bar to close outside consolidation range after P5. Volatility expansion signal.

**Where it appears:** Post-consolidation, after binary event resolution

**Macro trigger:** Catalyst resolves uncertainty (FOMC delivers decision, deal signing confirmed)

**Visual markers:**
- Bar closes outside range
- Often with volume spike
- Follow-through bars confirm direction

**Entry signal:** Breakout bars themselves (if volume confirms)

**Exit signal:** If breakout fails and re-enters consolidation range

**Engine state:** EXPANSION or DISTORTION (depending on direction alignment with macro thesis)

**Probability in brief:** HIGH if macro catalyst resolved as expected, DISTORTION if opposite

**Example:** Post-FOMC decision bar breaks out of pre-decision range

---

**P7 — Failed Breakout**

**Definition:** Bar that breaks above/below level but closes back inside, suggesting weakness. Reversal or retest risk.

**Where it appears:** At invalidation zones, when macro thesis breaks

**Macro trigger:** Trump denial tweet, deal collapse begins, unexpected hawkish FOMC

**Visual markers:**
- Extended wick beyond breakout level
- Body closes back inside prior range
- Often with decreasing volume on close

**Entry signal:** SELL signal if failed breakout is on upside. BUY if on downside at support.

**Exit signal:** Confirmation bar that retests the level or closes further in reverse

**Engine state:** FAILED or DISTORTION

**Probability in brief:** HIGH if macro thesis has binary event risk (deal could collapse, FOMC could surprise)

**Example:** NQ breaks 30,336 on deal collapse rumor but closes back above = P7 failed breakdown, buyers step in

---

**P8 — Divergence Resolution**

**Definition:** Two correlated instruments (NQ and GC) move in opposite directions. When divergence resolves, strong move follows.

**Where it appears:** Post-macro catalyst, when risk-on/risk-off regime shifts

**Macro trigger:** Safe-haven demand changes (deal clarity = GC down, NQ up)

**Visual markers:**
- NQ making higher closes, GC making lower closes (or opposite)
- Divergence widens over 3-5 bars
- Resolution occurs when one instrument fails to extend divergence

**Entry signal:** Entry into NQ momentum while GC weakness holds (and vice versa)

**Exit signal:** When divergence resolves (GC stabilizes while NQ rallies = end of setup)

**Engine state:** TRAVERSAL with uncovered thesis

**Probability in brief:** HIGH in macro-driven sessions (geopolitical moves)

**Example:** NQ up 150pts, GC down $50/oz = strong divergence, ride NQ until GC stabilizes

---

**P9 — Support/Resistance Bounce**

**Definition:** Price touches horizontal level (support or resistance), bounces away without breakdown/breakout.

**Where it appears:** At memory zones, at previous swing highs/lows

**Macro trigger:** Traders positioned at level (stops above/below)

**Visual markers:**
- Touch or near-touch of level
- Wick extends to level
- Close on opposite side of level

**Entry signal:** Bounce entry = buy at support (sell at resistance)

**Exit signal:** If price breaks and closes past level (support breaks)

**Engine state:** COMPRESSION or early TRAVERSAL

**Probability in brief:** HIGH at identified memory zones or HTF rails

**Example:** Memory zone at 30,336 tested, bounces up = buyers present

---

**P10 — Range Extension**

**Definition:** Bar that extends range (new high or new low) but doesn't break structure. Growth within existing framework.

**Where it appears:** Mid-trend, within strong momentum sequences

**Macro trigger:** Macro thesis confirmed, traders scaling in

**Visual markers:**
- New high or new low bar
- Often closes on opposite side of where it started intrabar
- Volume usually elevated

**Entry signal:** Add to existing position (scaling in)

**Exit signal:** If extension stalls and reversal bar follows

**Engine state:** EXPANSION within TRAVERSAL

**Example:** NQ breaks 30,545 = range extension, add to long position

---

**P11 — Exhaustion Spike**

**Definition:** Large candle (often 2-3x normal range) that closes opposite to its direction, showing exhaustion of move. Reversal signal.

**Where it appears:** At resistance (upward spike) or support (downward spike)

**Macro trigger:** First 30min after gap (P12 News Fade), or near target zones

**Visual markers:**
- Very large candle body
- Opposite close direction (spike up, close low)
- Often preceded by smaller consolidation

**Entry signal:** Reversal entry (fade the spike)

**Exit signal:** If spike continues in spike direction (rare, means strong continuation despite exhaustion appearance)

**Engine state:** EXHAUSTION

**Probability in brief:** HIGH in first 30min post-gap, HIGH at resistance zones

**Example:** NQ gaps up Mon, first 30min shows exhaustion spike (up 100pts, closes near mid-range) = P12 News Fade

---

**P12 — News Fade**

**Definition:** Spike (often P11 exhaustion spike) followed by reversal and drift lower. Market initially reacts to news, then fades reaction.

**Where it appears:** First 30min after major news catalyst, earnings gaps, central bank decisions

**Macro trigger:** News catalyst released, initial spike, then reality check

**Visual markers:**
- Spike candle (P11) followed by 2-3 bars of reversal and lower closes
- Volatility reducing as pattern completes
- Close near midpoint of spike range or lower

**Entry signal:** Reversal entry after spike completes (fade the news)

**Exit signal:** If prices stabilize and consolidate (fade complete)

**Engine state:** EXHAUSTION → COMPRESSION

**Probability in brief:** HIGH in news-driven sessions (geopolitical, central bank)

**Example:** NQ Mon 09:00 Tokyo gap-up spike, first 30min shows P12 fade (buyers realize deal may have limits), pullback to support

---

**P13 — Trap Resonance (vX.14 Detector)**

**Definition:** Wick sweep that extends into prior bar's range, triggering stops, then reverses. Detected by vX.14 Pine Script.

**Where it appears:** At support/resistance, at memory zones, at HTF rails

**Macro trigger:** High participation zone, stops clustered

**Visual markers:**
- Lower wick extends below support (or upper wick above resistance)
- Wick body does not extend (close is away from wick)
- Next bar often reverses from wick

**Entry signal:** Trap resonance = entry signal in direction of reversal (buy if lower wick trap at support)

**Exit signal:** If wick is broken completely and price sustains beyond

**Engine state:** COMPRESSION or early TRAVERSAL after trap

**Probability in brief:** HIGH at identified memory zones (high participation)

**Example:** Memory zone 30,336 tested with wick, vX.14 signals trap, next bar reverses upward

---

**P14 — Hidden Support/Resistance**

**Definition:** Price level not obvious from current timeframe structure but shows rejection when tested. Often from HTF.

**Where it appears:** Within larger ranges, multi-day levels

**Macro trigger:** Macro structure, not news

**Visual markers:**
- Unexplained rejection wick at certain level
- Multiple touches over multiple days at same price
- Not visible in memory zones (hidden)

**Entry signal:** Identify via vX Rhythm Engine multi-timeframe sync (5m/60m/240m)

**Exit signal:** If level breaks after clear rejection sequence

**Engine state:** COMPRESSION or DISTORTION (if level breaks)

**Probability in brief:** MEDIUM (requires HTF context, not pure macro)

**Example:** NQ rejects at 30,550 repeatedly but no obvious daily resistance there = hidden support from 60m structure

---

**P15 — Acceleration into Target**

**Definition:** Bars getting progressively larger/more volatile as price moves toward identified target. Conviction building.

**Where it appears:** Within momentum phases, as price approaches exit target

**Macro trigger:** Macro thesis unfolding, traders scaling in, conviction building

**Visual markers:**
- Each bar larger than prior in same direction
- Volume increasing
- Wick rejection decreasing

**Entry signal:** Add to position, not initial entry

**Exit signal:** When acceleration stalls or target reached

**Engine state:** TRAVERSAL with EXPANSION component

**Example:** NQ approaching 30,620 target, last 3 bars show acceleration upward (larger bodies, bigger range)

---

**P16 — Deceleration into Resistance**

**Definition:** Bars getting progressively smaller/less volatile as price approaches resistance. Conviction fading, exhaustion signal.

**Where it appears:** As price approaches resistance or target

**Macro trigger:** Traders profit-taking, uncertainty before binary event

**Visual markers:**
- Each bar smaller than prior
- Volume reducing
- Wick rejection increasing (indecision)

**Entry signal:** Reversal entry (fade approach to resistance)

**Exit signal:** If bars reverse and move back down, or if deceleration reverses and acceleration resumes (rare)

**Engine state:** EXHAUSTION

**Probability in brief:** HIGH approaching resistance or FOMC (traders waiting)

**Example:** NQ approaching 30,700 upper rail, bars decelerate (getting smaller) = exhaustion risk, reduce size

---

**P17 — Volume Confirmation**

**Definition:** Move accompanied by volume surge on close. Conviction signal.

**Where it appears:** At breakouts, at PRIMARY entries, at target reaches

**Macro trigger:** Macro thesis confirmed, broad participation

**Visual markers:**
- Bar close aligned with move direction
- Volume bar noticeably larger than recent average
- Often 2-3 consecutive bars with elevated volume

**Entry signal:** Volume on breakout = confirm trade is real

**Exit signal:** If move continues but volume drops to zero (conviction lost)

**Engine state:** TRAVERSAL with conviction (not COMPRESSION)

**Probability in brief:** HIGH if macro catalyst is unambiguous

**Example:** PRIMARY entry at 30,480 with volume surge = confirmation, add to position

---

**P18 — Volume Divergence**

**Definition:** Price move not confirmed by volume (move up on low volume or vice versa). Weakness signal.

**Where it appears:** Failed breakouts, near exhaustion, false signals

**Macro trigger:** Traders not committed, stop-hunt patterns

**Visual markers:**
- Price moving in one direction
- Volume below average
- Often precedes reversal

**Entry signal:** Reversal entry (fade high-volume-lacking move)

**Exit signal:** If volume surges and confirms move direction (flip)

**Engine state:** FAILED or DISTORTION

**Probability in brief:** MEDIUM (requires volume data integration)

**Example:** NQ breaks 30,545 on low volume = failed breakout risk, wait for volume confirmation

---

**P19 — Rail Cycle**

**Definition:** Price oscillates between upper and lower HTF rails, testing each in sequence. Compression pattern within defined bounds.

**Where it appears:** In consolidated markets, within larger structure

**Macro trigger:** Macro bounds established (upper cap = FOMC risk, lower bound = support)

**Visual markers:**
- Multiple touches of upper rail
- Reversal and test of lower rail
- Pattern repeats within defined range

**Entry signal:** Buy at lower rail, sell at upper rail (range trading)

**Exit signal:** If price breaks outside rails (new structure)

**Engine state:** COMPRESSION or TRAVERSAL within bounds

**Probability in brief:** LOW in strongly trending weeks (NQ Jun 16-20), HIGH into binary events

**Example:** Pre-FOMC Wed, NQ oscillates 30,620–30,680 (rail cycle), traders range-trade

---

**P20 — Session Liquidity Sweep**

**Definition:** Price extends in one direction early session, then reverses to sweep prior session's range or high/low, collecting stops.

**Where it appears:** London open Mon, after Asian session, start of US session

**Macro trigger:** New session begins, different participant set

**Visual markers:**
- Early extension in one direction
- Sharp reversal back
- Sweep through prior session's extremes

**Entry signal:** Entry in direction of reversal (after sweep completes)

**Exit signal:** If sweep continues beyond collected stops

**Engine state:** COMPRESSION → TRAVERSAL

**Probability in brief:** HIGH at session transitions

**Example:** Tokyo session closes 30,700, London open extends to 30,730, then sweeps back through 30,700–30,680 collecting stops, rallies again
