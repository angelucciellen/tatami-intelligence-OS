# TATAMI OS Lexicon

## Version 1.0

*"Language shapes understanding. Understanding shapes intelligence."*

---

# Purpose

The TATAMI Lexicon defines the official vocabulary of the platform.

Every document, AI agent, developer, researcher, and future contributor should use these definitions consistently.

The objective is to create a shared language for Behavioral Market Intelligence.

Words are not interchangeable.

Each term has a precise meaning inside TATAMI.

---

# A

## AI Skill

A specialized artificial intelligence capability designed to perform one specific responsibility within TATAMI, such as event classification, pattern discovery, replay analysis, or documentation.

---

# B

## Behavior

The observable expression of market activity resulting from the interaction of participants, liquidity, volatility, structure, time, and context.

Behavior is the fundamental unit of analysis within TATAMI.

The Mechanism does not predict price.

It classifies behavior.

---

## Behavioral Classification

The process of translating raw market observations into structured behavioral understanding.

A classification combines multiple layers of evidence into one coherent interpretation.

---

## Behavioral Intelligence

Understanding created by combining behavioral classifications, historical knowledge, context, and research.

Behavioral Intelligence explains markets rather than predicting them.

---

## Behavioral Narrative

A structured explanation describing what the market is expressing, why it is behaving that way, and which scenarios are currently most probable.

Narratives are produced by Renaissance.

---

# C

## Classification

The act of assigning a behavioral interpretation to observed market conditions based on evidence rather than opinion.

---

## Confidence

A quantitative measure representing how strongly current evidence supports a behavioral classification.

Confidence is dynamic.

It increases or decreases as new evidence becomes available.

---

## Context

The collection of environmental conditions surrounding an event.

Context may include:

* Macro conditions
* Session
* Liquidity
* Volatility
* Correlation
* Institutional positioning
* Economic events

Context influences interpretation but is never interpreted in isolation.

---

# D

## Discovery

A newly observed behavioral relationship identified through research.

A discovery becomes knowledge only after validation.

---

## Distortion

A behavioral condition where market activity becomes inconsistent due to elevated uncertainty, abnormal liquidity behavior, or external shocks.

---

# E

## Event

A single classified market occurrence recorded by TATAMI.

Events become the primary research unit inside the GT Framework.

---

## Evidence

Objective observations supporting or contradicting a behavioral interpretation.

Evidence may originate from:

* Statistics
* Historical research
* Structural analysis
* Macro Intelligence
* Replay
* The Mechanism

---

# I

## Intelligence

The synthesis of behavior, knowledge, memory, and context into coherent understanding.

Intelligence does not generate predictions.

It generates understanding.

---

# K

## Knowledge

A validated behavioral discovery permanently stored within Memory.

Knowledge represents information that has survived research and statistical validation.

Knowledge is reusable.

Knowledge compounds over time.

---

## Knowledge Object

The atomic unit of permanent knowledge inside TATAMI.

Every validated discovery becomes a Knowledge Object containing its evidence, confidence, relationships, version history, and supporting research.

---

# M

## Memory

The permanent knowledge repository of TATAMI.

Memory preserves validated discoveries, replay data, historical events, and behavioral relationships.

Unlike temporary analysis, Memory continuously expands.

---

## The Mechanism™

The proprietary behavioral classification system at the core of TATAMI OS.

The Mechanism translates market activity into behavioral understanding.

It does not predict markets.

It classifies them.

---

# O

## Observation

A factual description of market behavior without interpretation.

Observations always precede hypotheses.

---

# P

## Pattern

A recurring behavioral structure repeatedly observed across multiple events.

Patterns require validation before becoming knowledge.

---

## Pattern Recognition

The continuous process of identifying recurring behavioral relationships across historical observations.

---

## Pressure

The directional influence currently acting upon market behavior.

Pressure represents behavioral force rather than price direction.

Pressure may strengthen, weaken, or reverse as new evidence appears.

---

# R

## Renaissance

The intelligence layer of TATAMI OS.

Renaissance transforms behavioral classifications, historical knowledge, macro context, and AI reasoning into visual decision support.

---

## Replay

The reconstruction of historical events for learning, validation, and comparison.

Replay allows researchers to revisit behavior exactly as it occurred.

---

## Research

The structured process of observing, testing, validating, and documenting behavioral market phenomena.

Research creates knowledge.

---

# S

## State

A temporary behavioral condition identified by The Mechanism.

States describe how the market is behaving rather than what price will do next.

Examples include:

* Traversal
* Compression
* Expansion
* Exhaustion
* Failed Traversal
* Equilibrium
* Distortion

---

## Strategy

A decision framework constructed from behavioral intelligence rather than isolated signals.

Strategies combine multiple behavioral layers into a coherent execution plan.

---

# T

## Tag

The validated outcome assigned to an event during research.

Tags measure what actually occurred rather than what was expected.

---

## Trap

A behavioral condition where apparent opportunity attracts participants toward low-probability decisions.

Traps frequently emerge during institutional liquidity collection.

---

## Trap Resonance

A recurring behavioral condition where multiple pieces of evidence indicate elevated probability of institutional liquidity-seeking behavior.

Trap Resonance does not predict failure.

It identifies conditions where behavioral caution should increase.

---

# V

## Validation

The process of confirming whether an observed relationship survives statistical testing and repeated observation.

Validation transforms discoveries into knowledge.

---

## Validator

The Renaissance subsystem responsible for evaluating confidence by comparing behavioral evidence against macro, structural, statistical, and contextual information.

---

# W

## Wisdom

The highest level of understanding inside TATAMI.

Wisdom is the responsible application of intelligence through disciplined human judgment.

Wisdom cannot be automated.

It belongs to the researcher.

---

# Core Relationships

Research creates Knowledge.

Knowledge builds Memory.

Memory strengthens Intelligence.

Intelligence supports Decision Making.

Decisions generate new Observations.

New Observations begin Research again.

This continuous cycle allows TATAMI to improve without losing previous understanding.

---

# Closing Statement

The purpose of this Lexicon is not simply to define terminology.

Its purpose is to establish a common language through which humans and artificial intelligence can collaborate to understand market behavior.

As TATAMI evolves, this Lexicon will evolve with it.

Every new concept introduced into the platform should first become part of the language before becoming part of the software.

---

*The Mechanism translates behavior.*

*The Lexicon gives that behavior a language."*
