# THE MECHANISM™

## Behavioral Classification System

Version 1.0

*"The market speaks through behavior. The Mechanism translates it."*

---

# Purpose

The Mechanism™ is the behavioral classification technology at the core of TATAMI OS.

Its purpose is to transform raw market activity into structured behavioral intelligence.

It does not predict the future.

It continuously answers one question:

> **"What behavior is the market expressing right now?"**

---

# Mission

The Mechanism exists to reduce uncertainty through observation.

Rather than forecasting prices, it classifies behavior.

This behavioral understanding becomes the foundation for every higher-level intelligence layer inside TATAMI.

---

# Philosophy

Price is not intelligence.

Indicators are not intelligence.

Predictions are not intelligence.

Behavior is intelligence.

The Mechanism converts price into behavior.

Behavior into knowledge.

Knowledge into intelligence.

---

# Core Responsibilities

The Mechanism performs six primary functions.

## 1. Behavioral Observation

Observe raw market activity without interpretation.

Inputs include:

* Price
* Structure
* Time
* Volatility
* Liquidity
* Context

---

## 2. State Classification

Determine the current behavioral state.

Examples include:

* Traversal
* Compression
* Expansion
* Exhaustion
* Failed Traversal
* Equilibrium
* Distortion

The state represents the current behavioral condition of the market.

---

## 3. Behavioral Classification

The Mechanism combines multiple dimensions:

Behavior =

State

*

Location

*

Context

*

Time

*

Behavioral Evidence

This produces a complete behavioral classification rather than a single signal.

---

## 4. Confidence Assessment

Every classification receives a confidence score.

Confidence is determined by:

* State quality
* Behavioral agreement
* Historical evidence
* Pattern consistency
* Context alignment

Confidence is dynamic.

It evolves as new information becomes available.

---

## 5. Pattern Recognition

The Mechanism continuously searches for recurring behavioral structures.

Examples:

* Trap Resonance
* Behavioral transitions
* Structural repetition
* Institutional patterns
* Session behavior
* Regime persistence

Validated discoveries become permanent knowledge.

---

## 6. Learning Feedback

Every completed event strengthens the system.

Observed outcome

Validation

↓

Knowledge

↓

Future classifications

Learning never stops.
